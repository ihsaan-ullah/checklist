Question # 2: Does the paper discuss the limitations of the work performed by the authors?

Author's answer: Yes

Author's justification: Yes in Section 6

LLM Review:

Feedback on Limitations Discussion

The paper does discuss limitations in Section 6, however, it could be improved by expanding on several key points:
  • Assumptions: The paper briefly mentions the assumption of "no interference" between reviewers and papers. This should be elaborated on, discussing potential batch effects and the impact on estimated review quality. Additionally, the paper relies on monotonicity and Lipschitz smoothness assumptions for partial identification. While the suitability of these assumptions is discussed, the potential consequences of their violation should be explicitly addressed.
  • Review Quality Measures: The paper acknowledges the potential limitations of self-reported expertise and confidence as measures of review quality. Expanding on this, the paper could discuss alternative measures like meta-reviews or consider exploring potential biases inherent in self-reported data.
  • Generalizability: The authors recognize the limitations of drawing conclusions based on data from only two venues. Suggestions for future work to examine the generalizability of the findings would strengthen the discussion.

Recommendations

  • Expand discussion on "no interference" assumption, including potential batch effects and limitations of the current analysis.
  • Further explore limitations of self-reported review quality measures, consider alternative measures or potential bias mitigation strategies.
  • Discuss generalizability concerns in more detail, propose specific avenues for future work to address them.
